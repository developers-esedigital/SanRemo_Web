HOW TO GET THERE
How to get Sanremo
  • By plane
  • By train
  • By car
The nearest airport is 50 km from Sanremo, Nice International Airport; then there is the 144 km far Genoa Airport. In Nice airport you can find taxi service servizio taxi Car Rental and Shuttle Bus (line 98) towards Nice Train Station from which you can reach Sanremo by train. A recently activated coach line with stops at Sanremo tollgate and Coach Station complete the offer. More info on NIce Airport site dall’aeroporto di Genovaor ask the Taxi Company in Sanremo calling +39 0184 541454to know about fees and special tariffs. Also from Genoa Airport you can find Car Rental service and a Shuttle Bus towardsGenoa Porta Principe Train Station from which you can reach Sanremo by train. In both there is a coach line from and towards airport.
Sanremo Train Station is is situated in Cavallotti Avenue, a few steps from the near Police station, the Town Hall and the central Matteotti Street. The station is served by regional, interregional and intercity trains and inside you can find the ticket office, both traditional and ticket vending machine. A taxipoint is just outside the station.. Bus stops are just outside along the main road. Info and book on Trenitalia site or ask for help at Sanremo Train Station – also for disabled assistence service- calling the number 0184 503032.
Sanremo is served by the motorway network A10/E80 Highway of the Flowers, tract Genoa-Ventimiglia . The tollgate out is on the western hills of the town and from the exit you can reach the town centre driving down the scenic Padre Semeria route or the near Solaro Rapellin street. A good alterantive route can be to use the Arma di Taggiaexit (first town after Sanremo towards Genoa). From there you can enter the by-pass road called Aurelia Bis to avoid traffic jam. Aurelia bis presents different exits distributed along various areas of the town: Valle Armea/Bussana Mare, San Martino, Hospital and the central Borgo Tinasso. You may choose to avoid the motorway and drive along the ancient Roman Aurelia route that links Sanremo from the West to the East. If you arrive from France along the Costa Azzurra A8/E80 Menton Motorway, you have to go straight on towards Ventimiglia- Sanremo until you reach the exit.
